Use a "Password Manager"

  • Use a password manager. Simplist is your browser (Chrome or Firefox).
  • Let it generate passwords (never reuse passwords)

The Problem (Reuse)

  • Example login

Most people reuse passwords. That’s how accounts get taken over. 

Email: demo@example.com
Password: Password123

This is what people actually do. It’s unsafe.

Let Chrome Generate

  • Right-click password field
  • Select “Suggest strong password”

You don’t create passwords anymore. The browser does.

Save Password

  • Submit login
  • Click “Save” when prompted

Chrome stores it securely. You don’t need to remember it.

  • Return to login page

Next time, it fills it in automatically.

Use "Two-Factor Authentication" (2FA)

use two factor (2FS) authentication when available for even more security

what is it

factor of authentication Piece of evidence used to verify your identity during log-in

What are the different kinds of factors?

  • Knowledge factor – something you know, like a password or PIN
    • Easiest to hack – there are only so many possible combinations of numbers, letters, and symbols you can use.
  • Possession factor – something you own, like an email or a smartphone
    • Much harder to hack into an account if you also have a code sent to a phone to confirm that it’s really you.
  • Inherence factor – a part of you (facial ID or fingerprint)
    • Hardest to hack – almost impossible to fake a fingerprint or part of yourself, making this the strongest security option of the three.

Why Not SMS

  • Phone numbers can be hijacked
  • Codes can be intercepted
  • Attackers ask for codes

Use an "Authenticator App" when possible

for your 2nd factor, prefer use an authenticator app (e.g. Google Authenticator) instead of SMS (text messaging)

Review these Four Rules

  • Never click links in unexpected messages
  • Never send money or gift cards on request
  • Never share codes or passwords with anyone
  • Verify using a known contact method (not the message)

Keep this blunt and fast. This is the only slide they need to remember. Do not elaborate. Reinforce that partial adoption is still valuable, but these are the targets.

If Unsure

  • Do nothing
  • Verify independently
  • Scams = pressure
  • Rules = defense
  • Tools help, rules decide
