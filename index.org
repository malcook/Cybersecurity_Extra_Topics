* Use "Two-Factor Authentication" (2FA) 

use two factor (2FS) authentication when available for even more security
** what is it
factor of authentication
Piece of evidence used to verify your identity during log-in

** What are the different kinds of factors?

- Knowledge factor – something you know, like a password or PIN
  - Easiest to hack – there are only so many possible combinations of numbers, letters, and symbols you can use.
- Possession factor – something you own, like an email or a smartphone
  - Much harder to hack into an account if you also have a code sent to a phone to confirm that it’s really you.
- Inherence factor – a part of you (facial ID or fingerprint)
  - Hardest to hack – almost impossible to fake a fingerprint or part of yourself, making this the strongest security
    option of the three.

** Why Not SMS
- Phone numbers can be hijacked
- Codes can be intercepted
- Attackers ask for codes

* Use an "Authenticator App" when possible

for your 2nd factor, prefer use an authenticator app (e.g. Google Authenticator) instead of SMS (text messaging)

with an  Authenticator App
- Codes generated on device
- Not tied to phone number
- Works offline
